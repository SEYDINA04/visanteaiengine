Republic of Ghana, Ministry of Health, Ghana National Drugs Programme (GNDP), Standard Treatment Guidelines, 7th Edition, 2017.

Sample excerpt for RAG indexing when the full PDF is not yet available.

Fever and malaria: In malaria-endemic areas, assess for malaria in all febrile patients. Use rapid diagnostic test (RDT) or microscopy when available. Treat uncomplicated malaria according to national guidelines. Paracetamol for fever relief.

Respiratory: Upper respiratory tract infections are common. Most are viral. Antibiotics are not routinely indicated. Consider amoxicillin only when bacterial infection is suspected (e.g. prolonged fever, purulent discharge). Cough: symptomatic treatment; avoid codeine in children.

Pain: Use step-wise analgesia. Paracetamol first line for mild to moderate pain. NSAIDs (e.g. ibuprofen) for inflammation. Opioids only when necessary and under clinical supervision.

Chest pain: Assess for cardiac and other serious causes. Refer urgently if acute coronary syndrome or pulmonary embolism suspected. Do not delay emergency care for chest pain with dyspnoea or collapse.

Difficulty breathing: Treat cause. Severe respiratory distress is an emergency; ensure airway and oxygenation; refer urgently. Asthma: bronchodilators and inhaled corticosteroids per guidelines.

Bleeding: Apply direct pressure for external bleeding. Significant or uncontrolled bleeding requires emergency care. Assess for shock and anaemia.

Consciousness: Altered consciousness or fainting requires urgent assessment. Exclude hypoglycaemia, severe infection, neurological events. Refer to emergency care when indicated.
